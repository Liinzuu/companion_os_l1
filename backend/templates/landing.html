Companion OS™

A steady presence.
For when life gets hard.

Companion OS™ is an AI tool that teaches coping skills, helps you through difficult moments, and keeps you company. It is not therapy. It is not a diagnosis. It is something to try when you need a place to start.

Request access

Who it is for

If you're trying to get through something difficult. And the usual ways don't work anymore.

This is for the parts of life that don't come with instructions. The decisions you keep circling. The mornings that feel too heavy. The seasons that ask more than you knew how to give. You don't need a label to be welcome here. A few examples of who finds it useful:

  • People managing anxiety, overwhelm, or emotional dysregulation
  • People who find it hard to focus, start tasks, or stay on track
  • Teenagers navigating hard seasons without enough support
  • People in recovery from addiction, trauma, or a difficult period
  • Brain injury survivors and veterans rebuilding daily life
  • People facing a diagnosis or news that changed the path they were on
  • Elderly people living alone who need a calm, reliable presence
  • Anyone who just needs somewhere to start when they feel stuck

Companion OS does not diagnose conditions, prescribe treatment, or replace professional help. It teaches skills and keeps you company.

18 conversation modes

You do not need to know what mode you need. Just say what you feel.

Companion listens and responds in the way that fits the moment. Here are some examples of what that looks like.

I cannot start anything. I have been sitting here for an hour.

Focus Mode. Breaks the first step into something small enough to actually do

I am overwhelmed and I cannot breathe.

Calm Mode. Slows things down, grounds you in the present moment

I keep going in circles with this decision.

Decision Mode. Helps you see the options clearly and find your own answer

I need to understand this article but nothing is going in.

Study Mode. Breaks the content into the smallest useful pieces

I am having a craving and I do not know what to do.

Steady Mode. Helps you wait it out. The feeling always passes

Someone keeps crossing my limits and I do not know how to say stop.

Boundary Mode. Helps you name the limit and find the words

These are 6 of 18 modes. The full list: Calm, Mindfulness, Steady, Reality Check, Focus, Planning, Decision, Reflection, Boundary, Daily Companion, Study, Read, Listen, Express, Feedback, Habit and Aim, Relationship, and Help.

Impact survey

Four questions. At the start and after four weeks.

These questions are not about scoring you. They are about understanding whether the tool actually makes a difference in daily life. Not just whether it feels nice to use.

Each question is rated 1 to 5, from Rarely to Almost always.

  1. In the past week, I felt able to handle difficult moments in my day.
  2. I notice when my stress or emotions are building up before they become overwhelming.
  3. When I feel stuck or overwhelmed, I have something I can try that helps.
  4. I have been able to get through my daily tasks and responsibilities.

The same four questions appear again at the four-week mark, with one more added: Overall, I feel more steady and grounded in my daily life than I did a month ago. Your answers are anonymous and go directly into shaping the next version of Companion OS.

The pilot

Transparency

What Companion OS collects. And what it never does.

What we collect. With your consent.

  • Your messages, to have a conversation
  • Anonymous usage data, to understand what helps
  • Survey responses, to measure real impact

What we never do

  • Never diagnose or prescribe anything
  • Never share your data with third parties
  • Never store data without your explicit consent
  • Never use your data to train AI models
